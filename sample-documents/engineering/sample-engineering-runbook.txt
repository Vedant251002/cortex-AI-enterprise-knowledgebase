Atlantic Fictus Corp — Production Incident Response Runbook (Demo v1.0, fictional)

This runbook describes the fictional on-call process for the CloudWidget Suite platform.
Severity levels range from SEV-1 (full outage, customer-facing) to SEV-4 (cosmetic, no
customer impact). Any SEV-1 or SEV-2 incident must be acknowledged by the on-call engineer
within five (5) minutes of the PagerDuty (fictional) alert firing, or it automatically
escalates to the fictional secondary on-call rotation.

The standard triage sequence is: (1) acknowledge the page, (2) post an initial status update
in the #incidents channel within ten minutes, (3) identify whether the issue is isolated to
the Northfield or Lakeside fictional data center, (4) engage the CloudWidget service owner if
the root cause is not immediately obvious. Rolling back the most recent deploy is the default
first mitigation for any SEV-1 that started within thirty minutes of a release window.

Post-incident, a blameless retrospective must be scheduled within three fictional business
days for any SEV-1 or SEV-2. The retrospective document should capture: timeline, customer
impact in fictional affected-account-count, root cause, and at least one concrete follow-up
action with an owner and due date. Retrospectives are stored in the fictional Engineering
Wiki under the "Incidents" space and are visible to the whole engineering organization.

Database migrations affecting the fictional primary CloudWidget datastore require a second
engineer's sign-off and must be run during the designated low-traffic window of 02:00-04:00
in the Northfield data center's local time zone, except for emergency hotfixes.
